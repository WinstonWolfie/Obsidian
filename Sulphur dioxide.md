In [[susy]], sulphur dioxide and [[oxygen]] can be [[roaster|roasted]], with [[vanadium pentoxide]] as catalyst, to produce [[sulphur trioxide]]. Sulphur trioxide can be further [[mixer|mixed]], [[blender|blended]] or [[bubble column reactor|bubble column reacted]] with [[water]] to produce [[sulphuric acid]]. A slightly more convoluted, but more efficient way is to mix or blend sulphur dioxide with [[air]] to get [[Glover tower mixture]]. See more: [[Lead chamber process]].
