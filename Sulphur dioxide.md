In [[susy]], [[sulphur]] dioxide is a byproduct of many industrial processes, for example [[roaster|roasting]] [[pyrite]] or [[acanthite]]. It can be used to produce [[sulphur trioxide]], which can in turn be used to produce [[sulphuric acid]]. A more efficient way involves [[mixer|mixing]] or [[blender|blending]] sulphur dioxide with [[air]] to get [[Glover tower mixture]]. See more: [[Lead chamber process]].
