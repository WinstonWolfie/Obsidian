Informally, there exists a set $X$ with infinitely many members; these members are built by repeatedly applying the operation $S$ starting from the empty set.

In modern notation:

$$
\exists X \left [\varnothing \in X \land \forall y (y \in X \Rightarrow S(y)  \in X)\right].
$$

It's an axiom of [[Zermelo–Fraenkel set theory with the axiom of choice|ZFC]].
