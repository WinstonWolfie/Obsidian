An algebraic number is a number that is a root of a non-zero [[polynomial]] in one variable with [[integer]] coefficients.

Algebraic [[complex number]]s are closed under four [[elementary arithmetic]]s, thus forming a [[field]], denoted $\overline {\mathbb Q}.$ The set of algebraic [[real number]]s is also a field.

The field of algebraic numbers is the [[algebraic closure]] of the field of [[rational number]]s.
