The term domain, according to [[Wolfram MathWorld]], has (at least) three different meanings.

Mostly commonly, it's said to describe the set of values $D$ for which a [[function]] is defined.

Wolfram adds:

> Unfortunately, the term [range](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/Range.html) is sometimes used in probability theory to mean _domain_ (Feller 1968, p. 200; Evans _et al._ 2000). To confuse matters even more, the term "range" is more commonly used in statistics to refer to a completely different quantity, known in this work as the [statistical range](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/StatisticalRange.html). As if this wasn't confusing enough, Evans _et al._ (2000, p. 6) define a [probability domain](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/ProbabilityDomain.html) to be the _range_ of the _[distribution function](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/DistributionFunction.html)_ of a [probability density function](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/ProbabilityDensityFunction.html).
