In [[susy]], [[sulfur]]ic [[acid]] is important for making [[battery|batteries]]. A primitive way (already doable in [[steam age]]) to make it is to [[mixer|mix]] [[sulfur trioxide]] with [[water]]. A more efficient way involves [[Glover tower mixture]], called [[lead chamber process]].

However, in real life, directly dissolving $\ce{SO_3}$ in water, called the "[[wet sulfuric acid process]]", is rarely practiced because the reaction is extremely [[exothermic]], resulting in a hot aerosol of sulfuric acid that requires condensation and separation.

In susy, to produce sulfuric acid in an industrial scale from [[sulfur dioxide]], one would need [[Glover tower mixture]], with a process called [[lead chamber process]].
