Tuples in [[Python]] are used to store multiple items in a single [[variable (programming)|variable]]. For example,

```
mytuple = ("apple", "banana", "cherry")
```

It's [[ordered set|ordered]] and [[immutable]]. Tuple is one of 4 built-in [[data type]]s in Python used to store collections of data, the other 3 are [[List]], [[Set]], and [[Dictionary]].

In ordinary language, "tuple" is a back-formation from words like "single", "double", "triple", "quadruple", "quintuple", "sextuple", etc. It comes from [[Latin]] *plus*, related to [[Greek]] *‑πλοῦς*. Cf *[[ploidy]]*, from the Greek for *-fold*.
