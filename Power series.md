A power series is an [[infinite series]] of the form

$$
\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n \left(x - c\right)^n = a_0 + a_1 (x - c) + a_2 (x - c)^2 + \dots
$$

where $c$ is a constant called the centre of the series.

[[Borel's theorem]] implies that every power series is the [[Taylor series]] of some smooth function.

When the centre $c$ is zero, it becomes

$$
\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \dots,
$$

for instance in [[Maclaurin series]].
