Gluconeogenesis is the synthesis of [[glucose]] from non-[[carbohydrate]] sources.

Two major sources are:

- [[amino acid]]s, especially glucogenic amino acids like [[alanine]] and [[glutamine]]
- [[glycerol]], ie, the backbone of [[triglycerides]], released during [[lipolysis]] in [[adipose tissue]]; note that [[even-chain fatty acid]]s cannot be used, but [[odd-chain fatty acid]]s can be converted to [[propionyl-CoA]], which can enter gluconeogenesis.
