If [[field]] $\overline F$ contains all elements that are algebraic over $F,$ then $\overline F$ is an algebraic closure of $F$.

Two examples of algebraic closure:

1. The field of [[complex number]]s $\mathbb C$ is the algebraic closure of the field of [[real number|reals]] $\mathbb R$.
2. The field of [[algebraic number]]s $\overline{\mathbb Q}$ is the algebraic closure of the field of [[rational number]]s $\mathbb Q$.
