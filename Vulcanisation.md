Vulcanisation is a range of processes for hardening [[rubber]]s. The term originally referred exclusively to the treatment of [[natural rubber]] with [[sulfur]] and heat, which remains the most common practice. It has also grown to include the hardening of other (synthetic) rubbers via various means. Examples include [[silicone rubber]] via room temperature vulcanising and [[chloroprene rubber]] (neoprene) using metal oxides.

The term *vulcanisation* is derived from [[Vulcan]], the [[Roman god]] of fire, who was associated with heat and sulfur in [[volcano]]es.
