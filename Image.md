For a [[function]] $f: X \to Y$, the image is a relation between inputs and outputs.

The image of a [[subset]], using [[set-builder notation]], is defined as:

$$
f[A] = \{f(x) : x \in A\},
$$

which induces a function $f[\cdot]: \mathcal{P}(X) \to \mathcal{P}(Y)$, where $\mathcal{P}(S)$ is the [[power set]] of a set $S$.

The image of a function $f$ is the image of its entire [[domain]], aka the [[range]] of the function. This usage should be avoided because the word "range" is also commonly used to mean the [[codomain]] of $f.$

According to [[Wolfram MathWorld]], "image" is a synonym for "range", but "image" is the term preferred in formal mathematical writing. Also, the notation $f([a,b])$ denotes the image of the interval $[a,b]$ under the function $f$. Formally,

$$
f([a,b]) = \{y : \exists \ x \in [a,b] \text{ such that } f(x) = y\}.
$$
