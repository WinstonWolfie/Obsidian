According to [[Wolfram MathWorld]]:

> [[Hyperbolic geometry]] (or Lobachevsky-Bolyai-[[Gauss]] [[geometry]]) and [[elliptic geometry]] (or [[Riemann]]ian geometry). [[Spherical geometry]] is a non-Euclidean two-[[dimension]]al geometry. It was not until 1868 that Beltrami proved that non-Euclidean geometries were as logically [[consistency|consistent]] as [[Euclidean geometry]].

Naturally, [[Euclid's fifth postulate]] is not an [[axiom]] here.
